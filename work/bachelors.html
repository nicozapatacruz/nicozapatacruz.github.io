Overview

Where design thinking and engineering became one thing.

Product Design Engineering at EAFIT sits at the intersection of industrial design, mechanical engineering, electronics, and market strategy. Every semester ended with a physical product — prototyped, engineered, and presented. These four projects represent the progression from furniture design to autonomous robotics.

Selected projects

Four semesters, four built things.

2nd Sem.
A1-R3 — Camera Storage Furniture

A piece of furniture for a travel blogger — stores camera accessories in internal drawers and features a micro-perforated lid for scent diffusers on top. The name reads as both a camera lens model and "AIRE" (air) in Spanish. Developed through observation, collage, scale models, and technical blueprints.

3rd Sem.
Mythos — Dispenser

A dispenser inspired by the Versace Eros perfume bottle, capable of holding and delivering at least 30 items. The brief focused on conceptualisation — finding inspiration in an existing form and translating it into a fully functional object.

5th Sem.
Tiny — Electric Tricycle

A recreational electric tricycle powered by a 250W motor, steered using a tilting system. Built with 3D modeling, physical prototyping, structural simulations, and machining. The project combined mechanical engineering with ergonomic and aesthetic design decisions.

7th Sem.
STAF — Autonomous Road Marking Robot

An autonomous robot that assists road painters by tracing figure outlines on the pavement, leaving a dashed mask that the painter follows. STAF stands for Servicio de Trazado Automático de Figuras. Developed through multidisciplinary collaboration integrating electronics, mechanics, and programming.
